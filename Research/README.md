# Research

This folder contains research projects and technical contributions developed through university labs and academic collaborations. These projects involve applied engineering, prototyping, and experimentation in areas such as embedded systems, assistive technology, and intelligent environments.

Each research folder includes a detailed README describing the project goals, technologies used, and contributions.

---

## Folder Contents

### TOP Lab
Research conducted through the **Technology for Occupational Performance (TOP) Lab**, focusing on assistive technology and smart environments designed to support older adults.

Projects include the development of smart home training systems and modular demonstration kits used to simulate real-world smart home interactions. These systems are designed to help study fall prevention, technology adoption, and accessibility in aging populations.

The work involves hardware prototyping, smart device integration, and the development of modular systems that can be used for both research and training purposes.

---

### Warren B. Nelms Institute
Research contributions through the **Warren B. Nelms Institute for the Connected World**, focusing on embedded systems and intelligent sensing technologies.

Projects in this area involve the design and implementation of systems that combine sensors, embedded computing, and real-time processing to support intelligent real-world applications.

> **Note:** Much of the current work, source code, and detailed implementation is not publicly available due to ongoing research and publication processes.
